# Portfolio Construction Framework: Pokemon Card Prop Trading Desk

> Designed for a $10,000 starting bankroll trading ungraded Pokemon TCG singles on TCGPlayer.
> All return targets are net of platform fees (TCGPlayer: ~15.5% all-in on a $20 card).

---

## 1. Asset Allocation Buckets

### Core Holdings (40-50% of portfolio value)

**Purpose:** Anchor the portfolio with blue-chip cards that have secular appreciation tailwinds. These positions compound quietly while the trading book generates active returns.

| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Target allocation | 40-50% ($4,000-$5,000 at start) |
| Hold period | 6-24 months |
| Expected gross return | 15-30% annualized |
| Net return after fees | 5-18% annualized (sell-side fees eat ~15%) |
| Turnover | Low (1-2 trades per quarter) |
| Position sizing | $200-$800 per card |

**What qualifies as Core:**
- Tier 1 Pokemon: Charizard, Pikachu, Mewtwo, Mew
- Tier 2 Pokemon: Umbreon, Rayquaza, Lugia, Gengar, Eevee, Blastoise
- Rarity: Illustration Rare, Special Illustration Rare, Secret Rare, vintage holos
- Set status: Out of print or final print run
- Regime: ACCUMULATING or early UPTREND on the screener
- Liquidity score > 60 (must be sellable when thesis plays out)

**Example Core positions:**
- Charizard ex (Obsidian Flames SIR) — $80-$120, Tier 1 Pokemon, high collector demand
- Umbreon VMAX Alt Art (Evolving Skies) — $150-$250, trophy modern card
- Pikachu VMAX (Vivid Voltage SIR) — $60-$100, Tier 1, set out of print
- Mewtwo VSTAR SAR (Pokemon 151) — $40-$70, Tier 1, limited print set

---

### Growth (20-30% of portfolio value)

**Purpose:** Capture asymmetric upside from catalysts. These cards have identifiable reasons to appreciate within 1-6 months but carry more uncertainty than Core.

| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Target allocation | 20-30% ($2,000-$3,000 at start) |
| Hold period | 1-6 months |
| Expected gross return | 25-60% on winners |
| Win rate target | 55-65% (not every thesis plays out) |
| Turnover | Medium (2-5 trades per month) |
| Position sizing | $100-$400 per card |

**Growth catalysts to screen for:**
1. **Set rotation / out of print:** Card from a set entering its last print wave. Supply tightens, prices rise. Check Pokemon TCG set release calendars.
2. **Velocity acceleration:** Sales per day increasing while price is flat = demand building before price moves. Use the screener's velocity trend.
3. **Pokemon popularity surge:** New anime arc, video game feature, competitive meta shift. Tier 2-3 Pokemon getting Tier 1 attention.
4. **Rarity re-rating:** Market recognizing a card's pull rate is lower than initially thought. Usually shows up as steady price increase + low inventory.
5. **Set is undervalued relative to peers:** Same rarity/Pokemon but 30%+ cheaper than comparable cards in other sets.

**Kill criteria for Growth positions:**
- Catalyst didn't materialize within expected window: exit at market
- Velocity decelerating for 2+ consecutive weeks: trim or exit
- Better opportunity with same capital: rotate

---

### Trading (20-30% of portfolio value)

**Purpose:** Generate active returns by exploiting bid-ask spreads, velocity mismatches, and short-term mean reversion. This is the engine that funds losses elsewhere and builds pattern recognition.

| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Target allocation | 20-30% ($2,000-$3,000 at start) |
| Hold period | 1-30 days |
| Expected gross return per flip | 20-40% |
| Net return per flip (after fees) | 3-20% (fees are the enemy here) |
| Win rate target | 65-75% (high selectivity) |
| Turnover | High (5-15 trades per month) |
| Position sizing | $15-$150 per card |

**Trading signals (use the platform's spread analysis + velocity):**
1. **Spread flip:** Buy at market price when spread to median sold is > 20% AND velocity > 0.5 sales/day. Sell at median sold price. The fee math: need at least 25% gross spread to clear ~15% round-trip fees and net a profit.
2. **Velocity spike buy:** Card suddenly doing 2x+ its normal daily sales rate while price hasn't moved yet. Buy, ride the demand wave, sell into strength.
3. **Mean reversion:** Card dropped 15%+ in a week on no news (seller undercutting, not structural). Buy if liquidity score > 50 and historical median supports higher price.
4. **New listing arbitrage:** Same card listed at significantly different prices across conditions or sellers. Capture the gap.

**Hard rules for the Trading book:**
- Never hold a trading position longer than 30 days. If it hasn't worked in 30 days, cut it.
- Minimum $15 card price for trades (fee math doesn't work below this on TCGPlayer).
- Required: velocity > 0.3 sales/day. If nobody is buying the card, you can't flip it.
- Max 8 concurrent trading positions (attention is the bottleneck).

---

### Cash Reserve (10-20% of portfolio value)

**Purpose:** Dry powder for dislocations, new set releases, and avoiding forced selling of Core positions.

| Parameter | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Target allocation | 10-20% ($1,000-$2,000 at start) |
| Deploy when | Portfolio composite score > 75 opportunity appears AND cash > 10% |
| Rebuild to | Minimum 10% after any deployment |

**When to deploy cash aggressively (go to 10% floor):**
- Market-wide selloff (new set release cannibalized older set prices)
- High-profile Pokemon announcement creating temporary panic or euphoria
- Multiple screener candidates scoring > 80 composite simultaneously
- Tax season dip (February-April) when casual sellers liquidate

**When to build cash above 15%:**
- No screener candidates above 70 composite
- Portfolio win rate dropped below 50% for trailing 30 days (something is off, slow down)
- Macro uncertainty in collectibles market

## 2. Diversification Rules

These are hard constraints. No position enters the portfolio if it would violate any rule.

| Rule | Limit | Rationale |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Max single card | 8% of portfolio | A reprint announcement can crater any single card 30%+ overnight |
| Max single set | 20% of portfolio | Sets can fall out of favor together (rotation, reprint) |
| Max single Pokemon | 15% of portfolio | Pokemon-specific risk (new alt art crashes old version's price) |
| Max single era | 50% of portfolio | Vintage, modern, and ultra-modern have different demand drivers |
| Min positions | 10 | Below this you're gambling, not managing a portfolio |
| Max positions | 25 | Above this you can't track velocity/spreads on each card properly |
| Max single platform | 80% of inventory listed | Don't put everything on TCGPlayer; keep some on eBay as hedge |
| Min liquidity score | 30 for any position | Can't own cards you can't sell |

### Era Definitions

| Era | Sets | Characteristics |
|-----|------|-----------------|
| Vintage | Base Set through ex-era (1999-2007) | Low supply, high nostalgia, illiquid but strong floor |
| Modern | Diamond & Pearl through Sword & Shield (2007-2023) | Mixed liquidity, some trophy cards, most sets forgotten |
| Ultra-Modern | Scarlet & Violet onward (2023+) | High liquidity, volatile, most inventory lives here |

**Recommended starting mix:** 10-20% vintage, 30-40% modern, 40-50% ultra-modern. Shift toward modern/vintage as portfolio grows and you can afford less liquid positions.

## 3. Entry Framework: Composite Scoring Model

Every candidate card gets scored 0-100 before purchase. This replaces gut feeling with systematic evaluation.

### Component Scores

#### Fair Value Gap (30% weight)

How far below fair value is the current market price?

| Condition | Score |
|-----------|-------|
| Current price <= 70% of estimated fair value | 100 |
| Current price = 75% of fair value | 80 |
| Current price = 85% of fair value | 60 |
| Current price = 90% of fair value | 40 |
| Current price = 95% of fair value | 20 |
| Current price >= fair value | 0 |

**Fair value estimation:** Use the platform's median sold price over 30 days as the anchor. Adjust upward if velocity is accelerating (demand > supply balance shifting). Adjust downward if velocity is decelerating.

Formula: `fair_value_gap_score = max(0, min(100, (1 - current_price / median_sold_30d) * 333))`

This gives 100 at 30%+ below fair value, 0 at fair value, linear in between.

#### Velocity Trend (20% weight)

Is sales velocity accelerating, stable, or decelerating?

| Condition | Score |
|-----------|-------|
| 7d velocity > 14d velocity by 50%+ | 100 |
| 7d velocity > 14d velocity by 25% | 80 |
| 7d velocity roughly = 14d velocity | 50 |
| 7d velocity < 14d velocity by 25% | 20 |
| 7d velocity < 14d velocity by 50%+ | 0 |
| Zero sales in 7 days | 0 |

Formula: `velocity_score = max(0, min(100, 50 + (velocity_7d / velocity_14d - 1) * 100))`

#### Spread Analysis (15% weight)

Can you buy below where cards are actually selling?

| Condition | Score |
|-----------|-------|
| Market price < median sold by 25%+ | 100 |
| Market price < median sold by 15% | 75 |
| Market price = median sold | 40 |
| Market price > median sold | 0 |

Key: The spread must clear round-trip fees (~15.5% on TCGPlayer for a $20 card, ~13% for a $50 card) to be profitable. A 10% spread is a loss after fees. Use the platform's net proceeds calculator.

Formula: `spread_score = max(0, min(100, (median_sold - market_price) / market_price * 400))`

#### Set Dynamics (15% weight)

What is happening with this card's set?

| Condition | Score |
|-----------|-------|
| Set confirmed out of print | 90-100 |
| Set in final print wave (announced) | 70-85 |
| Set > 12 months old, no reprint signals | 50-65 |
| Set actively in print, 6-12 months old | 30-40 |
| Set just released (< 3 months) | 10-20 (prices still settling) |
| Reprint announced for this card/set | 0 |

This requires manual assessment. Check Pokemon TCG news sources weekly. The screener can't automate this yet, so maintain a set status spreadsheet.

#### Pokemon Popularity (10% weight)

How collectible is this Pokemon independent of card specifics?

| Tier | Pokemon | Score |
|------|---------|-------|
| Tier 1 | Charizard, Pikachu, Mewtwo, Mew | 90-100 |
| Tier 2 | Umbreon, Rayquaza, Lugia, Gengar, Eevee, Blastoise, Venusaur, Dragonite | 70-85 |
| Tier 3 | Gyarados, Gardevoir, Tyranitar, Snorlax, Garchomp, Alakazam, Giratina, Arceus | 50-65 |
| Tier 4 | Other fully evolved starters, legendaries, popular competitive Pokemon | 30-45 |
| Tier 5 | Random mid-evolutions, common Pokemon, forgettable designs | 0-20 |

The platform already has these tiers in the screener (`BLUE_CHIP_TIER1`, `TIER2`, `TIER3`). Map directly.

#### Liquidity Quality (10% weight)

Can you exit this position when you need to?

| Condition | Score |
|-----------|-------|
| Liquidity score > 80 (sells within 2-3 days) | 100 |
| Liquidity score 60-80 (sells within a week) | 75 |
| Liquidity score 40-60 (sells within 2 weeks) | 50 |
| Liquidity score 20-40 (may take a month) | 25 |
| Liquidity score < 20 (illiquid, might not sell) | 0 |

Use the platform's computed liquidity score directly.

### Composite Score Calculation

```
composite = (fair_value_gap * 0.30) +
            (velocity_trend * 0.20) +
            (spread_analysis * 0.15) +
            (set_dynamics * 0.15) +
            (pokemon_popularity * 0.10) +
            (liquidity_quality * 0.10)
```

### Entry Decision Matrix

| Composite Score | Action |
|----------------|--------|
| 80-100 | Strong buy. Size up to max position limit. |
| 70-79 | Buy. Standard position size. |
| 60-69 | Watchlist only. Buy if composite improves or spread widens. |
| 50-59 | Pass. Not enough edge to overcome fees. |
| < 50 | Hard pass. |

### Pre-Buy Checklist (must all be true)

- [ ] Composite score >= 70
- [ ] No diversification rule violations after adding position
- [ ] Card price >= $15 (fee math floor for trading bucket) or >= $40 (core/growth)
- [ ] Liquidity score >= 30
- [ ] Verified card condition matches listing photos (for any purchase > $50)
- [ ] Checked for upcoming reprints or set reprints in next 3 months
- [ ] Net profit after fees > $3 (absolute floor, otherwise not worth the operational effort)

## 4. Exit Framework

Every position must have a predefined exit plan. No "hold forever and hope" positions.

### Take Profit Rules

| Bucket | Take Profit Trigger | Action |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| Core | Price > entry * 1.35 | Sell 50%, hold rest with trailing stop at +20% |
| Core | Price > entry * 1.60 | Sell remaining. Re-evaluate for re-entry at lower levels. |
| Growth | Price > entry * 1.25 | Sell 100% if catalyst has played out. Hold if catalyst still unfolding. |
| Growth | Price > entry * 1.50 | Sell 100% regardless. Take the win. |
| Trading | Price > entry * 1.20 | Sell 100%. Quick flip margin after fees is ~5-8%. That's the game. |
| Trading | Spread closed (market price caught up to median sold) | Sell 100% even if under 1.20x. The edge is gone. |

### Stop Loss Rules

| Bucket | Stop Loss Trigger | Action |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Core | Price < entry * 0.80 AND velocity declining for 2+ weeks | Sell 100%. Thesis may be broken. |
| Core | Price < entry * 0.70 | Hard stop. Sell regardless. Something structural changed. |
| Growth | Price < entry * 0.85 AND catalyst window passed | Sell 100%. |
| Growth | Price < entry * 0.75 | Hard stop. Sell regardless. |
| Trading | Price < entry * 0.90 OR 14 days elapsed with < 5% gain | Sell 100%. Capital is better deployed elsewhere. |
| Trading | 30 days elapsed regardless of P&L | Sell 100%. Trading book is for velocity, not patience. |

### Rebalance Triggers

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Single position > 10% of portfolio | Trim to 8% |
| Single set > 22% of portfolio | Trim weakest card in that set |
| Single Pokemon > 17% of portfolio | Trim weakest card for that Pokemon |
| Cash < 10% of portfolio | Sell lowest-conviction trading position to rebuild cash |
| Bucket allocation drifted > 10% from target | Trim overweight, add to underweight |

### Thesis Invalidation (Immediate Exit)

These override all other rules. Sell at market immediately:

1. **Reprint announced:** The exact card or a functionally equivalent version is confirmed for an upcoming set. Historical precedent: reprints can cause 20-50% drops.
2. **Data quality issue discovered:** You bought based on bad data (wrong variant pricing, stale sales data, etc.). If you can't trust your entry thesis, exit.
3. **Condition downgrade:** Received card is worse condition than listed. Dispute or relist at corrected price immediately.
4. **Platform risk:** TCGPlayer policy change that affects fees, shipping, or selling economics.

### Stale Position Rule

Any position held > 120 days with < 5% unrealized gain gets reviewed:
- If there's a clear catalyst ahead (set going out of print next month), hold.
- If no catalyst in sight, sell and redeploy capital. Dead money is the silent portfolio killer.

## 5. Rebalancing Cadence

### Weekly Review (30 minutes, every Sunday)

- [ ] Check all positions against stop-loss levels
- [ ] Check all positions against take-profit levels
- [ ] Review Trading book: any position > 14 days? Evaluate for exit.
- [ ] Scan screener for new candidates scoring > 70
- [ ] Update velocity readings for Growth positions (is catalyst still intact?)

### Monthly Review (2 hours, first weekend of month)

- [ ] Calculate portfolio value (use current market prices from the platform)
- [ ] Check all diversification rules (single card, set, Pokemon, era limits)
- [ ] Review bucket allocation vs. targets. Plan rebalance trades if drifted > 10%.
- [ ] Review win rate by bucket. If any bucket < 50% win rate over 3 months, diagnose.
- [ ] Update set dynamics scores (any sets going out of print? Any reprint announcements?)
- [ ] Review cash level. Rebuild if < 10%.

### Quarterly Review (half day)

- [ ] Full portfolio performance review: total return, Sharpe, max drawdown
- [ ] Compare realized returns vs. entry thesis for every closed position. What did you get right? What did you miss?
- [ ] Reassess Core vs. Growth vs. Trading allocation. As bankroll grows, Core should grow proportionally.
- [ ] Review fee drag. If fees > 8% of gross return, position sizes are too small or flip margins too thin.
- [ ] Update Pokemon tier ratings if market sentiment shifted (new Tier 1 emerging?)
- [ ] Adjust composite scoring weights if one component consistently doesn't predict outcomes.

## 6. Portfolio Metrics to Track

### Return Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Total Return | (current_value + realized_gains - total_invested) / total_invested | > 25% annualized gross |
| Net Return | Total return minus all fees, shipping, and losses | > 10% annualized |
| Sharpe Ratio | (portfolio_return - risk_free_rate) / stdev(weekly_returns) | > 1.0 |
| Max Drawdown | Largest peak-to-trough decline in portfolio value | < 15% |
| Win Rate | Winning trades / total trades | > 60% overall |

### Per-Bucket Metrics

| Metric | Core Target | Growth Target | Trading Target |
|--------|-------------|---------------|----------------|
| Win rate | > 70% | > 55% | > 65% |
| Avg hold time | 6-18 months | 1-4 months | 3-14 days |
| Avg gross return per trade | 25-50% | 20-40% | 15-30% |
| Avg net return per trade | 10-35% | 5-25% | 2-12% |
| Avg loss on losers | -15% | -20% | -10% |

### Operational Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Fee drag | Total fees paid / gross return | < 30% of gross (this is realistic for TCGPlayer) |
| Avg position size | Portfolio value / number of positions | $400-$1,000 as portfolio grows |
| Portfolio liquidity score | Weighted avg of all positions' liquidity scores | > 55 |
| Capital utilization | (Portfolio value - cash) / portfolio value | 80-90% |
| Time to close | Avg days from listing to sale | < 14 for Trading, < 30 for Growth |

### Fee Drag Reality Check

TCGPlayer fee math for common position sizes:

| Card Price | Buy Cost | Sell Gross | Total Fees (TCGPlayer) | Net Proceeds | Round-Trip Fee % |
|-----------|----------|-----------|----------------------|-------------|-----------------|
| $15 | $15.00 | $18.75 (25% markup) | $2.80 | $15.95 | 18.7% of gross |
| $25 | $25.00 | $31.25 (25% markup) | $4.44 | $26.81 | 14.2% of gross |
| $50 | $50.00 | $62.50 (25% markup) | $8.61 | $53.89 | 13.8% of gross |
| $100 | $100.00 | $125.00 (25% markup) | $16.92 | $108.08 | 13.5% of gross |

Key insight: Fees as a percentage of gross return decrease as card price increases. The $15-$25 range is the tightest margin territory. For the Trading book, prefer $25+ cards where the fee math is more forgiving.

| Growth | $2,000 (20%) | 3-4 cards | $500-$667 | $40-$150/card |
| Trading | $2,000 (20%) | 4-5 cards | $400-$500 | $20-$80/card |
| Cash | $2,000 (20%) | - | - | - |

#### Starter Core Positions (pick 3-4)

| Card | Est. Price | Why |
|------|-----------|-----|

| Umbreon VMAX Alt Art (Evolving Skies #215) | $180-$250 | Trophy modern card, out-of-print set, Tier 2 Pokemon trending Tier 1 |

| Mewtwo VSTAR SAR (Pokemon 151 #195) | $50-$80 | Tier 1 Pokemon, limited print run set, strong demand |

Start with 3 Core positions. The 4th can be added in Month 2 once you have a feel for the market.

#### Starter Growth Positions (pick 3-4)

Use the platform screener with these filters:
- Invest Score > 65
- Liquidity Score > 50
- Regime = ACCUMULATING or early UPTREND
- Price range $40-$150
- Pokemon in Tier 1-3
- Set > 6 months old

Look for cards where velocity is stable or accelerating but price hasn't moved yet. These are the coiled springs.

#### Starter Trading Positions (pick 4-5)

Use the platform's spread analysis with these filters:
- Spread to median sold > 20%
- Velocity > 0.5 sales/day
- Liquidity Score > 60
- Price range $20-$80
- Net profit after fees > $3

The Trading book is where you start. Even before deploying Core and Growth capital, do 3-5 spread-based flips with small positions ($20-$40 cards) to calibrate your understanding of:
- How long it takes to sell at your list price
- Actual fee impact vs. projected
- Shipping logistics and time commitment
- Whether the platform's spread data is accurate

### Phase 2: Calibration (Week 3-8)

After 3-5 completed trading flips:
- Review actual vs. projected net returns
- Adjust minimum spread threshold if fees are higher than expected
- Adjust velocity threshold if cards are sitting unsold
- Deploy Growth positions based on screener candidates
- Begin scaling Core positions

### Phase 3: Steady State (Month 3+)

- Portfolio should be 10-15 positions
- Cash reserve normalized to 10-15%
- Bucket allocation hitting targets (45/25/20/10)
- Weekly rebalancing rhythm established
- Tracking all metrics above

### What to Buy First (Priority Order)

1. **2-3 Trading positions ($20-$50 each).** Learn the market. Validate your edge. Total: $60-$150.
2. **1 Core position ($100-$200).** Anchor the portfolio with conviction. Total: $160-$350.
3. **2 Growth positions ($40-$100 each).** Diversify into catalyst-driven plays. Total: $240-$550.
4. **2 more Trading positions ($25-$60 each).** Keep the flip engine running. Total: $290-$670.
5. **1-2 more Core positions ($100-$250 each).** Build the foundation. Total: $490-$1,170.
6. **Keep deploying until 80% capital utilized** across 10-12 positions.

This order prioritizes learning (Trading first) before committing larger capital to longer-duration positions (Core).

## Appendix: Quick Reference Decision Trees

### Should I Buy This Card?

```
1. Composite score >= 70?
   NO  -> Pass
   YES -> Continue

2. Any diversification rule violated?
   YES -> Pass (or trim existing to make room)
   NO  -> Continue

3. Card price >= $15?
   NO  -> Pass (fee math doesn't work)
   YES -> Continue

4. Liquidity score >= 30?
   NO  -> Pass (can't exit)
   YES -> Continue

5. Net profit after fees > $3?
   NO  -> Pass (not worth operational effort)
   YES -> BUY. Assign to Core/Growth/Trading bucket based on hold thesis.
```

### Should I Sell This Card?

```
1. Thesis invalidated (reprint, data issue)?
   YES -> SELL immediately at market
   NO  -> Continue

2. Hit take-profit target?
   YES -> SELL (partial for Core, full for Growth/Trading)
   NO  -> Continue

3. Hit stop-loss level?
   YES -> SELL full position
   NO  -> Continue

4. Position > 10% of portfolio?
   YES -> TRIM to 8%
   NO  -> Continue

5. Trading position > 30 days old?
   YES -> SELL regardless of P&L
   NO  -> Continue

6. Held > 120 days with < 5% gain and no catalyst?
   YES -> SELL and redeploy
   NO  -> HOLD
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Fees are the #1 enemy.** TCGPlayer takes ~15% of a sale. Your edge must clear this hurdle. This means minimum 20-25% gross spread on trading flips and 30%+ appreciation targets for longer holds.

2. **Start with the Trading book.** Small, fast flips teach you the market mechanics before you commit real capital to Core positions. You'll learn more from 10 completed $30 flips than from buying one $300 card and waiting.

3. **Liquidity is non-negotiable.** A card you can't sell is not an asset, it's inventory. Every position must have a liquidity score > 30. The Trading book demands > 60.

4. **Diversification protects you from reprints.** One reprint announcement can crater a card 30-50%. No single card should be able to cause a > 4% portfolio drawdown (8% position * 50% drop = 4% portfolio impact).

5. **Rebalance mechanically, not emotionally.** Set the rules, follow the rules. The weekly/monthly/quarterly cadence prevents both panic selling and hope holding.

6. **Track everything.** You can't improve what you don't measure. The metrics in Section 6 are the minimum. After 3 months, you should know your win rate, avg hold time, and fee drag by heart.
